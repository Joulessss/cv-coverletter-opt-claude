# Examples: Bullets, Summaries, Hooks

Concrete patterns for the rewrites. These illustrate the *shape* of good output — adapt to the candidate's real facts, never copy the numbers.

## Bullet rewrites — Action Verb + Task + Measurable Result

**Weak:** Responsible for managing the data pipeline.
**Strong:** Built and maintained a Python/Airflow ETL pipeline processing 2M+ records/day, cutting analyst wait time from 6 h to under 15 min.

**Weak:** Worked on a machine learning model for the company.
**Strong:** Developed an XGBoost churn model (AUC 0.89) that flagged at-risk accounts 30 days early, informing retention plays across a 40k-customer base.

**Weak:** Helped with teaching duties as a TA.
**Strong:** Led weekly lab sections for 60 graduate students in stochastic processes; redesigned problem sets that lifted median exam scores 12 points year-over-year.

**Weak (academic):** Did research on cocoa quality.
**Strong (academic):** Designed a multimodal sensory characterization protocol for Colombian cocoa liquor, integrating e-nose and HPLC data across 40 samples; findings under review at *Applied Food Research*.

Note how each names the **tool/method**, the **scope/scale**, and a **result**. When a percentage is unknown, an honest scale ("across 3 plants", "for 60 students") still beats a vague verb.

## Questions to recover missing metrics
- "Roughly how many [users / records / students / samples / dollars] did this involve?"
- "Did it get faster/cheaper/more accurate — by about how much? A rough range is fine."
- "Over what time period? Solo or with a team — how big?"
- "What changed for the org because you did this?"

## Summary rewrites — field-native, no clichés

**Generic:** Hardworking and detail-oriented professional seeking to leverage my skills in a dynamic team environment.
**Job (data science):** Data scientist with 4 years turning messy industrial data into deployed models — time-series forecasting, MLOps, and uncertainty quantification — with a track record of shipping work that cut cost and downtime in manufacturing settings.
**Academic:** PhD candidate in chemical engineering working at the intersection of applied machine learning and multimodal sensor data, with peer-reviewed work in ML-based process modeling and a focus on interpretable, decision-grade methods for physicochemical systems.
**Freelance:** Independent R&D consultant who has delivered production ML and process-optimization systems for LATAM agro-industry clients, scoped tight and shipped on time, from data audit to deployed dashboard.

## Cover-letter hooks (never "I am writing to express my interest")

**Insight hook (job):** "When [Company] shipped [recent feature/news from B1 research] last quarter, it signaled a bet on [X] that most of the sector still treats as optional. That bet is exactly where my last three years have lived."

**Problem hook (job):** "Scaling [thing the company does] past [stage] usually breaks on [specific hard problem] — the same wall I spent 2023 dismantling at [prior org], where I [quantified result]."

**Fit hook (academic):** "Professor [Name]'s recent work on [topic from B1] poses the question my dissertation has been circling for two years: [specific question]. I'd like to bring my [method] to that problem in your lab."

## CTA closings (never "I look forward to hearing from you")

- "I'd welcome 20 minutes to walk through how I'd approach [named problem] in your first 90 days — I can share a short plan beforehand."
- "Happy to send the preprint and code behind the [result] above; I think it maps directly onto [lab/team]'s current direction."
- "I'm available to start [date] and can scope a paid pilot on [deliverable] so you can judge the work before committing — shall we set a call?"

## Academic: research-statement & motivation hooks (PhD / cotutelle)

**Research-statement problem hook:** "Interpretability methods for industrial ML are largely validated on benchmark data, not on the noisy, multimodal sensor streams that real process plants produce — the gap my doctoral work targets directly."

**Advisor-fit paragraph:** "Professor [Name]'s recent work on [topic, cite from B1] develops [approach]; my prior characterization of [candidate's result] suggests this could extend to [proposed direction]. I'd want to test that in your group, drawing on [specific lab resource/dataset/method]."

**Cotutelle bridge:** "The project needs both sides: [Institution A]'s [data/field access/domain expertise] and [Institution B]'s [computational method/instrumentation]. Neither alone closes the loop from [raw signal] to [decision-grade model]."

**Academic motivation-letter CTA:** "I'd be glad to send my current manuscript (under review at [venue]) and a two-page sketch of the proposed aims, and to discuss how they'd fit [lab]'s direction — would a short call in the coming weeks be possible?"

## Academic CV ordering
For Master's/PhD applications, order sections: Summary/Research Interests → Education → Research Experience → Publications & Preprints → Conference/Talks → Teaching → Technical Skills → Awards. Publications and research come *before* generic work history. Master's-level: if few publications, lead with research projects, thesis, and relevant coursework instead.

## Spanish CVs — action-noun form (NOT conjugated verbs)

In Spanish-language CVs, bullets use the action-noun (or impersonal) form, not a conjugated past-tense verb. Conjugated verbs read as third-person narration and are stylistically wrong on a CV.

**Wrong (conjugated):** Desarrolló un pipeline ETL en Python que procesó 2M de registros al día.
**Right (action-noun):** Desarrollo de un pipeline ETL en Python para el procesamiento de 2M de registros diarios, reduciendo el tiempo de espera de 6 h a menos de 15 min.

**Wrong:** Lideró sesiones de laboratorio para 60 estudiantes.
**Right:** Liderazgo de sesiones de laboratorio para 60 estudiantes de posgrado; rediseño de talleres que elevó la mediana de notas 12 puntos interanual.

**Wrong:** Implementó un modelo de churn con XGBoost.
**Right:** Implementación de un modelo de churn (XGBoost, AUC 0.89) que identificó cuentas en riesgo 30 días antes, informando estrategias de retención sobre una base de 40k clientes.

Common openers: Desarrollo de, Diseño de, Implementación de, Ejecución de, Liderazgo de, Optimización de, Construcción de, Análisis de, Coordinación de, Validación de, Caracterización de, Automatización de. Match the document language to what the posting/call expects.
